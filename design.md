# UMC Report Design

Reference form: `paper/ICT index_2025.pdf`.

This design guide adapts the visual grammar of the ICT Development Index report to the UMC Seoul methodology report without copying its proprietary cover artwork or institutional marks.

## Project Topic

An Integrated Methodology for Measuring Universal and Meaningful Connectivity (UMC) in a Hyper-Connected City

## Format

- Page size: the reference report uses A4 portrait; the active DOCX keeps its existing Letter portrait setup unless the full report is reflowed.
- Tone: institutional, technical, and restrained.
- Layout principle: large white margins, one strong blue title field, compact front matter, and clear hierarchy before the analytical chapters.
- Visual language: clean report typography, blue primary blocks, dark navy headings, small yellow accent marks for methodological highlights.

## Cover

- Top header: small report family label on the left and project/team context on the right.
- Main title field: a saturated blue horizontal band occupying roughly the upper third to middle of the page.
- Title treatment: white, bold, left-aligned title in two to three lines; keep the English project topic visible as the main subtitle.
- Lower visual field: use an original city/connectivity motif, preferably simplified isometric or block-based, to signal data infrastructure, everyday use, and urban scale.
- Footer: small institutional/report mark at the bottom right, with date and deliverable context kept secondary.

## Page 2 Key Results

- Title: `Key Results at a Glance`.
- Purpose: show only the main results before the report body, not the full method narrative.
- Structure: one short framing sentence plus four to six evidence cards.
- Result cards should cover:
  - Seoul's district-level UMC disparities across the six dimensions.
  - HLM finding that individual characteristics explain much of digital-use variation while district context still supports place-sensitive interpretation.
  - Platform-text analysis as exploratory lived-experience signal detection, not direct prevalence estimation.
  - Bayesian updating as a bridge between measured district conditions and platform-visible signals.
  - Administrative-neighborhood to district aggregation and living-population matching as the basis for spatial consistency.
  - Policy implication: combine people-based support with place-sensitive local targeting.

## Typography

- Main cover title: large, bold sans serif, white on blue.
- Chapter/page headings: dark navy, bold sans serif.
- Body text: readable sans serif or the existing DOCX body font; avoid decorative typefaces.
- Numbers and short result labels: bold navy or blue.

## Color Palette

- Primary blue: `#009FDB` or similar saturated cyan-blue.
- Deep navy: `#244A86`.
- Text black: `#222222`.
- Light background: `#F3F7FB`.
- Accent yellow: `#F7C948`, used sparingly.

## Constraints

- Do not use ITU logos, cover art, signatures, or institutional marks.
- Do not include raw platform text, post IDs, private text, or source-level examples in front matter.
- Keep the cover and key-results page independent from chapter numbering.
- Verify the final DOCX by rendering the first pages to PDF before release.
